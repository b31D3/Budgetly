## How It Works

### Step 1: Calculate Monthly Expenses
```
Academic Costs (spread monthly):
- Tuition + Books + Supplies (annual amount Ã· 12)

Living Costs (already monthly):
- Rent + Utilities + Groceries + Cell Phone + Transportation + Memberships

TOTAL MONTHLY EXPENSES = Academic Costs/month + Living Costs
```

### Step 2: Calculate Monthly Income
```
Work Income (varies by month):
- School months (Jan-Apr, Sep-Dec): School hours Ã— 4 weeks Ã— hourly rate
- Summer months (May-Aug): Summer hours Ã— 4 weeks Ã— hourly rate

Financial Aid (spread monthly):
- (Scholarship + Bursary + Grant) per semester Ã— 2 semesters Ã· 12 months

Savings (spread monthly):
- Total savings Ã· total months

TOTAL MONTHLY INCOME = Work + Aid/month + Savings/month
```

### Step 3: Calculate Net Monthly Cashflow
```
NET MONTHLY CASHFLOW = Monthly Income - Monthly Expenses
```

### Step 4: Track Cumulative Balance
```
Each month:
  Cumulative Balance = Previous Balance + Net Monthly Cashflow
```

## Example

**Student Profile:**
- 6 semesters
- $11,200 total annual costs ($933/month)
- $12,000 work income/year ($1,000/month average)
- $2,000 scholarship/semester ($333/month)
- $5,000 savings ($139/month over 36 months)

**Monthly Breakdown:**
```
Monthly Income: $1,000 (work) + $333 (aid) + $139 (savings) = $1,472
Monthly Expenses: $933
Net Monthly Cashflow: $1,472 - $933 = +$539/month
```
